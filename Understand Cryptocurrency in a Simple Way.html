What is Stored in a Crypto Wallet?

What you store and transfer from a crypto wallet is generally called cryptocurrency

What you store and transfer from a crypto wallet is generally called cryptocurrency, but there’s an important nuance that often gets misunderstood.

A crypto wallet doesn’t literally “hold” coins the way a physical wallet holds cash. Instead, it stores private keys, which give you access to assets recorded on a blockchain.

How It Works

  • Cryptocurrencies exist on a blockchain (a distributed ledger).
  • Your wallet stores private keys (like a secret password).
  • These keys allow you to:
    • Prove ownership of your crypto
    • Send (transfer) it to others
    • Access your balance on the blockchain
Important: When you “send crypto,” you’re not moving coins from your wallet. You’re authorizing a transaction on the blockchain that changes ownership.
Simple Analogy:
Blockchain = Bank ledger
Crypto = Money in the ledger
Wallet = Your debit card + PIN

Important Distinction

Not everything in a wallet is strictly cryptocurrency. Wallets can also store:

  • Tokens (like stablecoins or utility tokens)
  • NFTs (digital collectibles)

In general conversation, people use “cryptocurrency” to describe these transferable digital assets.
